How to use

Image credit to pond5

In this tutorial, we will cover how to set up the detector, how to take readings, and how to use results to improve your irrigation schedule.

Overview of the system

The electronic wetting front detector has two parts: the sensor and the reader.



The sensor is an inexpensive device shaped like a cylinder. It has metal rings at various depth to measure the resistivity of the soil, which the reader uses to calculate the wetting front depth. Many sensors can be deployed across the field to measure the wetting front at different locations. Sensors are passive and do not require any batteries. They need to be used in conjunction with the reader to measure the wetting front.

The reader is a battery powered electronic device that provides power to the sensor and uses its measurements to calculate the wetting front depth. Only one reader is needed to read from all the sensors. To take a reading, the user just needs to attached the reader to the top of the sensor, and the readerâ€™s LEDs will light up to indicate the wetting front depth. This will be described in more detail in the following sections

Setting up the sensor


Choosing the right location

For furrow irrigation, the water diffuses out from the furrow to the plants on the side. Therefore, the sensor should be placed with an offset from the furrow center, preferrably at the edge of the furrow.

For drip irrigation, all the water is being concentrated around the dripper with dry soil between the drippers. Therefore, the sensor should be placed beside the drip line.


Digging the hole for the sensor

The sensor requires a hole of 5cm diameter and 40cm depth. You can tell whether the hole is deep enough by putting the sensor in. If the hole has the right depth, the blue line on the sensor should be at ground level

Burying the sensor

When you are happy with the hole, push the sensor into the hole until the blue line is at ground level. Then pack in soil around the edge until you can no longer wobble the sensor in the hole.

Measuring with the reader

Reader controls

The reader has a simple user interface that consists of four depth indication LEDs, a power switch, a calibration button, and a power LED.

Note that the calibration button is not used in this prototype, but in the future it can be used to initiate a software calibration procedure


Attaching the reader to the sensor

To get a measurement from the sensor, first align the connector on the sensor with the holes on the reader.

The red highlighted parts should be aligned with each other:



Once in position, push the sensor down until you feel that the springs are tight. Then turn on the reader switch to take measurements. You will know that the reader is on when the power LED lights up.




Interpreting the reader result

When the wetting front reaches a certain depth, an LEDs on the reader will light up.

The relationship between the LEDs and the wetting front is as follows:

LED Wetting Front Depth (cm)
No LED 0 - 20
1 20 - 30
2 30 - 35
3 35 - 40
4 > 40

The following table shows the desired range of wetting front depths for different irrigation methods.

Irrigation Method Min Wetting Front Depth (cm) Max Wetting Front Depth (cm)
Drip 30 45
Furrow 20 30
Sprinkler 20 35
Flood 30 40

Maintenance and good practices


Protecting the sensor

The sensor can be left in the ground for many years. It will not corrode as the metal parts are made of stainless steel. However, over time the exposed parts of the wires could become more fragile through wear and eventually break, at which point the sensor would need to be replaced. To minimize the deterioration of the wires, try not to move or twist the sensor after it has been placed into the ground.

Reader battery replacement

The reader uses 3 AAA batteries and has an expected battery life of half a year. It is recommended that you switch the reader off while not using to minimise battery consumption. It is also possible (but not yet implemented) to use rechargeable batteries and charge the reader with mains electricity, if it is available.
