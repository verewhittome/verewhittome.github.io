Electrical Team

Design

We want to revolutionise agriculture

Wet soil conducts electricity considerably better than dry soil. Through measurements of soil resistivity, the electrical wetting front detector therefore allows farmers to accurately and cheaply determine when their crops have been fully irrigated. This massively reduces wastage of vital water resources.

The detector is split into two modules

The Sensor


The sensor rod contains the electrical probes necessary to distinguish dry soil from wet. The sensor consists of a PVC tube (durable, cheap and easy to source) as well as 4 pairs of conductive, metal, flat rings.
By sending a test voltage through each of the 4 sets of probes, and comparing the corrseponding outputs it is possible to clearly determine the height of the wetting front to a high degree of accuracy.

The Reader


Using just a small microprocessor and 4 LEDs, the reader simply and effectively communicates to the end user how deep in the soil the wetting front has reached. The reader interfaces with the sensor rod with a 'tap on/ tap off' design, rendering the product easy to use and extremely robust.

By keeping the design modular, the farmer is able to keep multiple sensors installed at various locations in his fields. It is then only necessary to have 1 reader which can be quickly moved across the field, taking readings at each of the sensor positions.
The modular design also protects the vulnerable electrical components in the reader device from exposure to the elements and can be stored safely within households.

UNICEF Principles of Digital Design

Image source: www.pexels.com

The detector was created with the UNICEF digital design principles in mind.

  • Working with the User
    • The interface was kept as simple as possible (4 LEDs) for users with limited experience of electronics
    • Our solution minimises the amount of physical labour required from the farmer
    • Modular design to make replacement of electrical components as simple as possible
  • Understand the existing ecosystem
    • Agriculture accounts for 46.3 percent of the nation's Gross domestic Product (GDP), 83.9 percent of exports, and 80% of the labour forcecite
    • The use of a wetting front detector has been shown to reduce water consumption in agriculture by approximately 50% (and increase yields)cite
  • Design for scale
    • Modularity of the sensor and reader allows for multiple sensors to a single reader, covering a wide area
    • With the exception of the 3D printed parts (only used for prototyping purposes), the design is easily mass manufactured
  • Build for Sustainability
    • The reader uses very low power (assuming 10 minutes use every day, the batteries would last approximately 180 days)
    • Robust design of the pipe and sensors
  • Be data driven
    • Literature review in the brainstorming phase
    • Throughough testing of the prototype
  • Use open standards, open data, open source and open innovation
    • Manufacture and user manual specifications on this site
  • Reuse and improve
    • Built upon the full stop wetting front detector but changed the fundamental technology
    • Recycling of pipe material to manufacture the sensor rod
  • Be collaborative
    • Task sharing between electrical and mechanical approach teams
      • Background research
      • Evaluation
      • Testing
      • Website
    • Consultation with another academic team tackling this task in Ethiopia
