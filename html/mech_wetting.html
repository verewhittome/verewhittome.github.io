Bouyant Wetting Front Detector

A fully mechanical, and low cost solution for appropriate irrigation

This device is a low cost and entirely mechanical solution which signals to farmers when their land has been irrigated to a given depth. This both ensures that crops are provided with the required amount of water, and that over-watering is avoided which is both wasteful and leads to soil erosion.

Usage

The wetting-front detector should be buried in the vicinity of the crops, such that its tip reaches the desired irrigation depth. In the following weeks and months the device need not be moved, farmers only need to reset it before every cycle of irrigation. A bright coloured indicator flag pops up at the top of the device , indicating that the wetting front has reached the depth of the sensor. This flag is held in place by a ratchet mechanism and can be reset by pressing down on it.

Device Overview

The device is composed of an underground and an overground section, connected by plastic tubing. The underground section is made up of a funnel, which collects water from the descending wetting-front and directs it into a central reservoir. At the other end of the device, a mechanism is located that displays the current state of the soil at the depth of interest.

Funnel

In order to increase the quantity of water collected from the irrigated ground, the funnel is filled with sand. As it turns out, sand can only absorb around 30% of its volume in water, which is significantly less than the 52% absorption capacity of the soil tested. Therefore sand will release water into the central section much easier, resulting in a shorter detection time.

Base

As the water begins to collect in the central reservoir, the buoy housed there begins to rise and pushes up the light rod connecting it to the signalling mechanism on top of the device. The water collected in the base needs to be removed every time the device is reset. This design implements a system of side channels, much like those found in a pythagorean cup, that ensure that the reservoir is freed of water. Once the water level reaches an overflow threshold, the excess begins to evacuate via a side channel. This creates a syphoning effect that fully empties the collection cavity at the base of the device.

Tip

The initially increasing water level represents the signal which has to be communicated to the farmers. The rising buoy “transmits” this signal, by pushing on a light rod. The other end of the rod is connected to a bright coloured “flag” which pops up at the top of the device as water levels rise. Keeping in mind that the base contains a self emptying mechanism, a ratchet system has been integrated into the tip of the device, that keeps the flag up even after the base emptied itself of water. The user only needs to gently press down on the tip to reset it. As the flag, and therefore the connecting rod and the buoy, are pressed down, the water level rises above the overflow even if it wasn’t the case before, ensuring that the device is actually reset.
