CUES Venture Competition Summer 2018

ABOUT

Inspiring Entrepreneurship

The CUES mission is to deliver the very best opportunities to students to enhance their skills and develop their employability. Furthermore, we aim to show our members the world of opportunity in engineering and technology available to them outside of their respective course.

CUES is lauching the Summer Venture Programme for 2 reasons:

  • We believe that there is a wealth of talent and entrepreneurialism in the Cambridge student body which currently doesn't have the support or opportunity to build the next generation of tech start-ups out of the Silicon Fen.
  • There has never been a greater in interest from employers in students who engage in their own 'side projects' or 'ventures'. It shows passion for engineering and separates interesting recruitment candidates from the sea of students who followed the grain and soley worked on their course.

As students ourselves, we understand the intense workloads during term and the difficulties with fitting in a venture project into the academic calendar. CUES is therefore launching the summer venture competition to offer students the chance to take advantage of the summer holidays to work on entrepreneurial, engineering-related projects.
The competition has been designed to be open-ended so as to include a wide range of interests in technology but also not to be so vague that its difficult to find a starting point. There are therefore 4 competition categories with 4 corresponding panels of experts and senior representatives from industry (See below for details).
We've partnered with these leaders in industry to adjudicate the competition, to provide technical help along the way to teams & to mentor projects that they are excited about into becoming (we hope!) fully fledged start-ups.

Why Sign Up?

  • Unique opportunity with support to create your own start-up
  • Develop your technical and entrepreneurial skills
  • Directly engage with leaders and experts in industry
  • Become a member of the CUES venture programme (certifiably CV'able)
  • Chance to win prize money + further mentorship for your project

Format

FORMAT

The aim of the competition is to provide individuals and teams with the financial and technical resources to facilitate creating their own venture. At the end of the 2 months, candidates will be judged on a demonstratable piece of engineering work or research they have produced and its entrepreneurial application.

Timeline

  • Competition Sign Up Opens. July 23rd 2018
    Initial sign ups for inidividuals and teams.
  • Competition Starts. August 10th 2018
    Teams and individuals begin working on projects.
  • Informal Review. September 2018
    Informal review with a member of the CUES team to check on progress, offer feedback and suggest more resources to support the project.
  • Final Review. Start October 2018
    Final review with Judges and CUES team to decide the short-list of projects to go through to the main competition day.
  • Competition Day. 13th October 2018
    Culmination of the projects. An opportunity to showcase the work of the teams and to hear some inspirational talks from some of our judge panelists.
Competition Categories

SMART CITIES

Building the cities of the future


Smart Cities.
A smart city is a network, predominantly composed of Information and Communication Technologies (IOT devices and Low Power Wide Area wireless technologies), to develop, deploy and promote sustainable development practices in order to address growing urbanization challenges. This framework consists of an intelligent mesh of connected objects and machines that transmit data using wireless and cloud technologies. Cloud-based IoT applications receive, analyze and manage data in real time to help municipalities, enterprises and citizens make better decisions that improve ubran efficiency and quality of life. Citizens can engage with smart city ecosystems in a variety of ways using smartphones and mobile devices, as well as connected cars and homes. Pairing devices and data with a city’s physical infrastructure and services can cut costs and improve sustainability. smart Cities projects could tackle energy distribution, streamline waste collection, decrease traffic congestion and even reduce crime rates with help from the IoT.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Ground breaking algorithms


Artificial Intelligence.
One of the biggest buzzwords of our generation, the definition of AI can vary wildly depending on its application. At the core of AI however, is the capability of a machine (in particualr a computer system) to emulate human intelligence.
Correlated with the rise of 'Big Data', AI & machine learning tools have never been more powerful for solving complex real world issues and the frontier for what is possible with these algorithms is still being explored.
It is only necessary to look at the number of AI applications in start ups and industry leaders to realise the wide range of projects that could be possible on this topic. Furthermore, high-level platforms such as Tensor Flow mean that is technology has never been more accessible to some with a passion for problem solving.
